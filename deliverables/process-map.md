# Process Map — Henderson & Associates Client Intake

| # | Step | Automate / Keep Human / Hybrid | Justification |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Receptionist (Tamika) answers inbound call and collects basic info: name, phone number, accident type, date | Hybrid | AI phone bot or intake form can capture structured data 24/7, reducing load on Tamika, but a human should remain available for distressed callers who need reassurance. |
| 2 | Tamika checks statute of limitations against the wall chart and determines if the case is within the filing window | Automate | SOL lookup is a rules-based calculation (date of accident + case type → deadline) with zero discretion required; automating it also eliminates the malpractice risk of a missed edge case. |
| 3 | Tamika transfers the call to Maria, or takes a message if Maria is unavailable | Hybrid | Routing logic can be automated (transfer if available, queue callback if not), but the human handoff moment matters for client experience given the firm's relationship-first culture. |
| 4 | Maria (or staff) calls the potential client back when she was unavailable at first contact | Automate | An automated callback or SMS acknowledgment sent within minutes would close the window competitor firms exploit; the firm is losing cases specifically because this step is slow. |
| 5 | Maria conducts screening call: asks ~8–10 questions about accident details, fault, witnesses, injuries, and treatment status | Hybrid | AI can run the standard screening script and score case strength, but Maria (or another human) should make the final keep/reject decision and handle emotionally distressed callers. |
| 6 | Maria manually searches the 15-year client spreadsheet for conflicts of interest | Automate | Conflict checks are a structured name-matching lookup and the current manual process carries disbarment-level malpractice risk; this is the highest-priority automation in the firm. |
| 7 | Maria enters all case details (contact info, accident facts, injuries) into Clio | Automate | Pure data entry from a structured intake form into a case management API; automating this saves Maria 15–20 minutes per case and eliminates transcription errors. |
| 8 | Maria sends the retainer agreement to the client via DocuSign | Automate | This step already works well as-is (Laura called it their "one technology win") and should remain fully automated. |
| 9 | Maria makes check-in calls at 7, 14, and 30 days post-onboarding to update clients and confirm they are keeping medical appointments | Hybrid | Automated SMS/email reminders can handle the 14- and 30-day touchpoints that currently fall off, while Maria owns the 7-day call where the human relationship is most critical. |
